Fred’s salary is $31,000. He isn’t thinking about saving for retirement because of student loans, but he doesn’t realize he’s missed $2,500 in matching money in just 2 years.

RETIREMENT SEEMS FAR AWAY

But you could be missing out on $1,000s in matching money from your agency right now if you aren’t contributing at least 5% of your salary to your TSP account. And you can never get that money back.

Fred is 30. If he begins contributing 5% now, he could have about $365,000* by age 65. If he waits until age 40 to save the same amount, he’d have about $180,000* by age 65. Wait until his late 40s, and he’d have around $90,000* by age 65.
*Includes matching contributions and assumes a 6% rate of return compounded monthly.

START NOW

Time is your biggest ally when it comes to the growth of your retirement savings. Wait too long, and it will be much harder to catch up. If you’re unsure where to start, why not Take FIVE for Your Future?

FIVE PERCENT

On top of your salary, your agency will also contribute up to 5% of your pay to your TSP account each paycheck. But you only get the full amount if you contribute at least 5% too. Can’t afford 5%? Every $1 makes a difference.
